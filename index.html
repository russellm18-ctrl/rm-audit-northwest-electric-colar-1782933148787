// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your installation gallery images—electrical, solar, and battery storage—are almost certainly served as decorative files with no embedded alt-text, no JSON-LD schema connecting them to your Tesla Powerwall installer status, and no EXIF location data tying installs to specific Washington service areas. Right now, a homeowner in Bellevue or Spokane typing "Tesla Powerwall installer near me" is landing on a competitor's page because your images carry zero geographic or product-specific indexing signals. Your NABCEP accreditation claim and Tesla Premier Certified Installer designation—both high-trust, high-intent keywords—exist nowhere in your image metadata stack where search engines can actually use them.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Facilities managers and high-equity homeowners evaluating a five-figure solar-plus-storage investment need to see finished system photos with visible panel layouts, clean conduit runs, and Powerwall mounting quality before they call. Your site claims "thousands of systems installed" and Washington's #1 ranking, but your installation galleries are the only visual proof point, and they carry no annotated context—no before/after pairs, no system size labels, no Washington neighborhood identifiers. That gap means your most qualified buyers—the ones who read specs and check certifications—are self-qualifying themselves out because the visual record doesn't match the written claim.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Established Pacific Northwest solar contractors like Sunbridge Solar are deploying geo-tagged, project-specific case study pages with labeled system configurations, utility bill comparisons, and homeowner testimonials embedded directly alongside installation photography. You are selling Tesla's rail-free, all-black aesthetic as a differentiator but showing no close-up photographic evidence of the minimalist mounting finish or the absence of exposed hardware that your copy explicitly promises. Every week that gap exists, a design-conscious Washington homeowner who Googled "all-black solar panels Washington State" clicks away to a competitor who shows them exactly what they're buying.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured asset capture protocol tied directly to your active install schedule—document the Tesla rail-free mount system at rough-in, completion, and 30-day follow-up with consistent angles, labeled metadata, and GPS coordinates embedded at the file level before upload. Each completed job becomes a georeferenced, schema-tagged project page: system size, Powerwall count, Washington city, NABCEP-certified installer credit, and Tesla Premier designation structured in JSON-LD so every image functions as a local search asset, not a gallery thumbnail. This turns your existing installation volume into a compounding indexed archive that outperforms paid search on high-intent queries without adding a single dollar to your ad budget.
